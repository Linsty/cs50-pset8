Thanks CS50, a quick review!

Nice that you ended up on this page.
Although this page is online and the content isn’t nonsense, I built this webpage as a study assignment for pset8 of the Harvard CS50 2021 course.
This course has taught me a lot and I can recommend it to anyone who wants to learn a little more than the standard tutorials that you can find everywhere on the internet.
I think it's a pretty tough course, but it's a lot of fun to do.
It’s almost mandatory to have good English skills if you want to attend to this course.
In general my English skill are fine but I had my struggles once in a while.

Although I still have some lessons to go, I am already quite far in the course. I therefore have the feeling that I will finish the course soon.

I think the structure of the lessons is very good. The lectures are fun but since 2021 they last between 2.5 and 3 hours.
David Malan is a fluent speaker and does not repeat endlessly. Thus you get a lot of information during this time.
I personally love that, but if you find this too hard, you can always pause the lesson.

If you are more lyrically oriented, you also can read back the entire colleague.
In case the curriculum was still a bit too hard for you, then Doug Lloyd's videos came in handy.
You can absolutely say the course offers you enough visual and textual materials.

If you think the lessons are tough, I can tell you that the assignments are often at least as tough.
In general, after reading the text and the clear videos of Brian Yu, I figured it out,
but sometimes I really didn't understand what to make at all. So the question how to make it wasn’t even there.
In those moments I felt like I couldn't speak English at all. But when I finally figured everything out I really was over the moon.

The course facilities are excellent. A well-functioning IDE (Integrated development environment), manuals, links to communities, etc.
Actually, I only missed 2 facilities.

  1. A facility where I could take notes. I find this very easy with courses in Udemy.
  2. A link to a manual for the terminal commands.

Obviously I am absolutely nitpicking, but if Harvard would add this in their course
I would grade them a 10 in a scale from 1 to 10 instead of the 9.5 now.

I'm not there yet. But thank you Harvard for this great experience!
